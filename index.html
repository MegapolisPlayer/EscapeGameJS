A byte (get it?) of trivia
  • Nezamyslice were originally going to be Němčice nad Hanou, a real village 6km away. It was decided to remake the location due to the real-life layout of railroad tracks.
  • All cities are real and in the Olomouc Region in the Czech Republic.
  • Studénka had 3 fatal railway accidents since 1990 - in 1990 a train hit a car and 1 person died, in 2008 a train hit a falling bridge (yes, really) killing 8 people and in 2015 a SC Pendolino train hit a truck killing 3 passengers.
  • Přerov's criminal history is a bit of a Czech meme.
  • With some exceptions (Nezamyslice house), all photos come from the location`s actual, real-life locations.
  • This game was originally planned in Scratch, however due to technical difficulties we ended up switching to JS. Good riddance if you ask me.
  • Out of all the school escape game projects, this one is the largest measuring in at >103 MB. It is also the only one fully playable on mobile, with a save system and the only one written in JS and published on GitHub.
Rant about Scratch by Martin

Don't get me wrong, Scratch is a wonderful way to teach children how to code. But for anything more complex than a spinning cat eating apples Scratch rapidly becomes a very slow and terrible experience. I will list my biggest gripes about Scratch:

  1. Size limit - 10MB for sound and 50MB total is just plain not enough for larger projects. (sure, compression exists but then the quality falls of a tall cliff)
  2. Lack of documentation - the Scratch wiki is horrendously outdated and usually doesn't have great explanations.
  3. Variables - local/global divide - you can't change or view the state of the variable (you kind of can view it but still)
  4. The cloning system - it's bad, nowhere does it denote whether it does or does not copy the local or global variables
  5. Quality, resolution - 480x360 is not really enough for most larger projects
  6. Flexibility - basically none, very few options for blocks
  7. Performance - what is optimization? Even for moderately complex projects the loading time is measured in minutes. (Even Python and JS (in which this is written)) are good in comparison).
  8. Collaboration - you can't unless you spend hours setting up Studios. Git/GitHub is simpler. (except for submodules and GitHub Actions but who cares))
  9. Licensing - I do sometimes like sharing my projects under something different CC-BY-SA 2.0.

In fairness though, Scratch is really a great tool for teaching young kids the basics of algorithms (if statements, for loops, cycles, variables...) and such but it just isn't designed for anything more complex.
